Explore Lingayen Capitol

A scenic coastal landmark in Pangasinan offering historic views, seaside walks, and stunning sunsets. Perfect for photographers, history buffs, and weekend travelers.

Learn More

About the Landmark

The Lingayen Provincial Capitol, completed in 1918 during the American colonial period, stands as a proud symbol of neoclassical architecture in Pangasinan. During World War II, the area became a major battleground as Japanese forces occupied the province and Allied troops landed on the shores of Lingayen Gulf in 1945. The capitol suffered heavy damage, yet the determination of the Pangasinense people led to its restoration—patiently rebuilt and revived to reflect its original grandeur. Today, it remains a testament to resilience, heritage, and the enduring spirit of the province.

How to Get There

By Bus
Take a bus from Dagupan to Lingayen. You can ride the bus in front of SM Dagupan. Most buses going to Lingayen will pass by the Lingayen Capitol. If the bus you take does not pass the Capitol, get off in front of PNB Lingayen and ride a tricycle going to the Lingayen Capitol. Travel time is around 40-60 minutes.
By Car
You can drive from Dagupan via De Venicia Road to avoid traffic in the main road to the Lingayen Capitol using two possible routes. The first option is the main road, which takes around 40–50 minutes depending on traffic and leads directly to the Capitol area. The second option is the route through Binmaley, where you pass by the Binmaley Baywalk and continue straight toward Lingayen. This route is slightly longer but offers a more scenic drive.

Activities

Photography & Sunset Watching

Capture stunning golden-hour views and scenic architectural angles perfect for photography.

Seaside Walks

Enjoy a peaceful walk along the coastal path while taking in the refreshing sea breeze.

Ukay-Ukay

Browse affordable second-hand clothes and unique finds from local ukay-ukay stalls.

Food Stalls

Taste fresh seafood and popular Pangasinan delicacies served by nearby vendors.

Ride ATV

Experience an adrenaline-filled adventure by riding an ATV across the sandy coastline (Price: ph250 for 15 minutes).

Perya

Enjoy carnival-style games, lights, and rides for families and friends.

Dancing Fountain

Watch colorful water displays synchronized with music during evening shows.
